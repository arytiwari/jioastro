# Comprehensive Vedic Yoga Database (251 Yogas)

**Date:** 2025-11-10
**Purpose:** Complete catalog of all classical Vedic yogas for systematic implementation
**Source:** BPHS (Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra), Phaladeepika, Jataka Parijata, Saravali

---

## Overview

This document catalogs all 251 classical Vedic yogas with:
- Canonical name (primary spelling)
- Known variations (spellings, alternate names)
- BPHS formation conditions
- Category (Major Positive, Major Challenge, Standard, Minor, Subtle Influences)
- Effects
- Detection logic

---

## Categorization System

### 1. **Major Positive Yogas** (40-50 yogas)
- Life-changing positive effects
- Create wealth, power, wisdom, or spiritual attainment
- Examples: Pancha Mahapurusha, Gaja Kesari, Raj Yogas, Dhana Yogas

### 2. **Major Challenge Yogas** (20-30 yogas)
- Significant obstacles or malefic effects
- Require remedies and conscious effort
- Examples: Kemadruma, Daridra, Grahan, Chandal, Arishta yogas

### 3. **Standard Yogas** (80-100 yogas)
- Moderate positive or negative effects
- Shape specific life areas
- Examples: Chandra yogas, Surya yogas, planetary combination yogas

### 4. **Minor Yogas** (60-80 yogas)
- Subtle effects on personality or life events
- Supporting role in overall chart
- Examples: Nitya yogas (27), house-specific yogas

### 5. **Subtle Influences** (40-60 yogas)
- Very subtle effects
- Nuance and refinement to predictions
- Examples: Specific planetary degree combinations, minor Nabhasa variations

## CATEGORIZATION LOGIC

### Major Positive Criteria:
- Creates significant wealth (Dhana yogas)
- Bestows power/authority (Raj yogas)
- Grants wisdom/learning (Saraswati, Buddhi)
- Ensures spiritual attainment (Moksha yogas)
- Classical "Maha" (great) yogas
- BPHS specifically mentions as very auspicious

### Major Challenge Criteria:
- Causes significant obstacles (Daridra, Kemadruma)
- Creates malefic effects requiring remedies (Doshas)
- BPHS specifically warns about effects
- Impacts core life areas negatively (health, wealth, relationships)

### Standard Criteria:
- Moderate positive or negative effects
- Shapes personality traits
- Influences specific life areas moderately
- Classical yogas without "Maha" prefix

### Minor Criteria:
- Subtle personality nuances
- Short-term effects
- Supporting role to major yogas
- Daily/monthly yogas (Nitya, etc.)

### Subtle Influences:
- Very minor effects
- Requires other yogas for activation
- Refinement and nuance only
- Divisional chart (Varga) specific
